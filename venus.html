Venus

Venus, often referred to as Earth's sister planet due to its similar size, is the second planet from the Sun in our solar system. It's often called the "morning star" or "evening star" due to its brightness, and it's one of the brightest objects in the night sky. Venus has a thick atmosphere primarily composed of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid, creating a runaway greenhouse effect that makes it the hottest planet in the solar system, even hotter than Mercury, despite being farther from the Sun. Its surface is rocky and dotted with volcanoes and vast plains, but its thick atmosphere makes it difficult to observe its terrain from space.
